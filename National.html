Char Dham

The Char Dham refers to the four sacred pilgrimage sites in Hinduism, located in the Indian state of Uttarakhand. These sites are considered extremely significant for Hindus, and millions of devotees undertake pilgrimages to these locations every year. Here's a brief overview of each of the Char Dhams:
1.Badrinath: Situated in the Chamoli district, Badrinath is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites in India. It is located at an altitude of 3,133 meters in the Garhwal Himalayas.
2.Kedarnath: Located in the Rudraprayag district, Kedarnath is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is situated at an altitude of 3,583 meters and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, sacred to Lord Shiva.
3.Gangotri: Gangotri is situated in the Uttarkashi district and is the source of the holy River Ganges. It is located at an altitude of 3,100 meters in the Garhwal Himalayas.
4.Yamunotri: Yamunotri is located in the Uttarkashi district and is the source of the Yamuna River. It is situated at an altitude of 3,293 meters in the Garhwal Himalayas.

Kashmir

Nestled in the lap of the Himalayas, Kashmir is a breathtakingly beautiful region known for its stunning landscapes, rich culture, and warm hospitality. Often referred to as "Paradise on Earth," Kashmir is a destination that captivates travelers with its snow-capped peaks, lush valleys, serene lakes, and vibrant cultural heritage.

1.Dal Lake: Explore the iconic Dal Lake on a shikara (traditional wooden boat) ride and marvel at the floating gardens, houseboats, and historic Mughal gardens lining its shores.

2.Gulmarg: Experience the thrill of skiing and snowboarding in winter or enjoy the lush meadows and breathtaking views of the surrounding peaks during the summer months in Gulmarg, the "Meadow of Flowers."

3.Srinagar: Immerse yourself in the charm of Srinagar, the summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir, known for its picturesque gardens, historic landmarks like the Shankaracharya Temple, and vibrant markets offering traditional handicrafts and spices.

4.Pahalgam: Trek through the scenic trails of Pahalgam, known as the "Valley of Shepherds," and visit attractions like Betaab Valley, Aru Valley, and the enchanting Lidder River.

5.Sonamarg: Discover the alpine beauty of Sonamarg, the "Meadow of Gold," surrounded by majestic glaciers, towering peaks, and pristine lakes, offering opportunities for trekking, camping, and adventure sports.
